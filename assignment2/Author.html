James Brown, also known as "Woz," is an American inventor, electronics engineer, and programmer who co-founded Apple Inc. with Steve Jobs and Ronald Wayne in 1976. Born on August 11, 1950, in San Jose, California, Wozniak showed an early interest in electronics and computers. He developed the Apple I, the company's first product, in 1976, single-handedly designing and hand-building the computer's motherboard. Wozniak's engineering prowess and innovative designs were instrumental in the success of Apple's early products, including the Apple II, which became one of the first highly successful mass-produced personal computers.

Beyond his technical achievements, Wozniak is renowned for his philanthropy, advocacy for computer education, and support for various charitable causes. Despite leaving Apple in 1985, he remains an influential figure in the technology industry and continues to inspire generations of engineers, entrepreneurs, and innovators worldwide. Wozniak's contributions to the development of personal computing and his entrepreneurial spirit have earned him numerous awards and honors, cementing his legacy as one of the most influential figures in the history of modern technology.
